# Revision Checklist

> Revision is not proofreading. It is re-envisioning. Each pass targets a different layer. Do NOT try to fix everything at once.

## Pass 1: Structural Edit

Read the full manuscript with these questions:

### Plot
- [ ] Does the inciting incident happen early enough?
- [ ] Does every act serve its structural purpose?
- [ ] Is the midpoint a genuine shift, not just another complication?
- [ ] Does the climax resolve the central conflict (not a surrogate)?
- [ ] Does the resolution feel earned, not convenient?
- [ ] Are there any scenes that can be cut without affecting the story?
- [ ] Are there missing scenes the story needs?

### Arcs
- [ ] Does the protagonist's arc complete (or intentionally fail to)?
- [ ] Does the antagonist have a coherent motivation and arc?
- [ ] Do secondary characters serve the story (not just fill space)?
- [ ] Are all character arcs resolved or intentionally left open?

### Stakes
- [ ] Are the stakes clear and escalating?
- [ ] Does the reader know what the protagonist stands to lose?
- [ ] Do the stakes feel personal, not just situational?

## Pass 2: Character Edit

Reread focusing only on characters.

- [ ] Is each character's voice distinct? (Cover the name -- can you tell who's speaking?)
- [ ] Are motivations clear and consistent?
- [ ] Do characters make decisions, or do things happen to them?
- [ ] Are emotional reactions proportional and earned?
- [ ] Do relationships evolve across the story?
- [ ] Is the protagonist active in the climax (not a bystander)?

## Pass 3: Pacing Edit

Read for rhythm and momentum.

- [ ] Does each chapter end with a reason to keep reading?
- [ ] Are there dead spots where momentum stalls?
- [ ] Is the ratio of scene (action) to sequel (reflection) appropriate for the genre?
- [ ] Does the pace accelerate toward the climax?
- [ ] Are quiet moments earned and intentional (not accidental)?
- [ ] Is exposition woven into action, not dumped?

## Pass 4: Continuity Edit

Check facts, timeline, and internal consistency.

- [ ] Is the timeline consistent? (Map it if needed)
- [ ] Do physical descriptions stay consistent?
- [ ] Are world rules obeyed? (No unexplained exceptions)
- [ ] Do characters know only what they should know?
- [ ] Are proper nouns consistent (spelling, capitalization)?
- [ ] Are seasonal/weather/time-of-day details consistent with timeline?

## Pass 5: Line Edit

Prose-level improvements, paragraph by paragraph.

- [ ] Cut filler words (just, really, very, quite, somewhat, rather)
- [ ] Eliminate crutch phrases and repetitive constructions
- [ ] Activate passive voice where it weakens prose
- [ ] Sharpen dialogue (cut pleasantries, add subtext)
- [ ] Strengthen verbs (replace "walked slowly" with "shuffled")
- [ ] Add sensory detail where scenes feel thin
- [ ] Cut sensory detail where it bogs down pacing
- [ ] Check for unintentional repetition (words, images, sentence patterns)

### AI-Generated Prose Patterns

If any portion of the manuscript was AI-assisted, check specifically for these common patterns:

- [ ] **Crutch words**: "particular," "something about," "a sense of," "the weight of" — search the manuscript and reduce by 60-70%
- [ ] **Filter phrases**: "she felt," "she noticed," "she realized," "she couldn't help but" — replace with direct sensation or cut
- [ ] **"The way" construction**: "the way [she/someone] [verb]" — varies some to direct description
- [ ] **"The kind of" construction**: "the kind of [noun] that [clause]" — often cuttable
- [ ] **Paragraph opener monotony**: Consecutive paragraphs starting with "She" or the POV character's name — lead with objects, sensory details, or actions instead
- [ ] **Over-explained metaphors**: A strong image immediately followed by a sentence explaining what it means — trust the first image, cut the explanation
- [ ] **Em-dash overload**: Long parenthetical asides in em dashes that should be their own sentences — break up if more than one per paragraph
- [ ] **Thought catalogues**: "She thought about X. She thought about Y. She thought about Z." at chapter endings — replace with fragmented images or sensory details
- [ ] **"Hummed/hum" repetition**: Ambient sounds described with the same verb across chapters — vary or reduce to one use per Part

## Pass 6: Read-Aloud

Read the entire manuscript out loud (or use text-to-speech).

- [ ] Flag sentences that make you stumble
- [ ] Flag dialogue that sounds unnatural spoken aloud
- [ ] Note rhythm problems (too many same-length sentences in a row)
- [ ] Mark passages that bore you (if they bore you, they'll bore the reader)
